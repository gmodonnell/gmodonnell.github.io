Cryptography

Cryptography is how we protect our information using a lot of complex math. Cryptography is what allows us to transmit sensitive information across insecure networks without having to lose sleep.

There are three types of cryptographic systems we operate: Symmetric Encryption, Asymmetric Encryption and Hashing

Symmetric Encryption Asymmetric Encryption Hashing
Uses one shared key to encrypt and decrypt messages Uses pairs of keys, one for encryption and one for decryption. One-Way functions that produce a fixed length output unique to the source message.
Fast. Less Computing. Slower. More Computing. Must be computationally impossible to reverse the process. Every input must generate a unique output.
Used for Messaging and Data Storage Used for key exchange authentication and digital signatures. Used for integrity checks, non-repudiation and digital signatures.
